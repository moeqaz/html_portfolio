About Me

I am currently 23 years old, pursuing a future in software development. Not long before enrolling for the 10-week coding bootcamp, offered
by Coding Temple, I often found myself feeling hopeless, with no direction in regards to a career. I was working at a Honda dealership and
barely had any time to think about what I can do in regards to working towards a career. A couple of my friends had taken the same course
offered by Coding Temple, and recommended me to look into it. Eager to turn my life around, I had started practicing coding on freecodecamp
and realized that, with a lot of practice, I can learn to code and build a promising future for myself. The car business wasn't going too well,
as there is an ongoing car shortage, so I figured that it was the best time to make a switch.
I was born in High Point, North Carolina and had moved to Baltimore, Maryland at the age of 8, and have been living here with my family
since. I am the oldest of 5, with 2 brothers and 2 sisters. Growing up in a big family, many would describe me as family oriented, as I
am very close with all of my family members, and never spare any opportunity to spend time with them.

My Background

I was born in North Carolina, but my family comes from Pakistan, which is located in Southeast Asia. I am my parents' oldest child, so growing
up, my parents spoke Urdu in the house, so it had become my primary language. When I enrolled in school, I didn't know how to speak any English,
and had to go through ESOL for the first couple of years. When I moved to Maryland, and started middle school, I found a few friends with similar
backgrounds, and we all played cricket together, a sport which is a huge part of the Pakistani culture.
As much as I really wish I went more often, I have only been to Pakistan twice. My mom, my 2 sisters and I moved back to Lahore for a year in 2005,
but decided to move back shortly after seeing the living conditions over there. My most recent visit to Pakistan was this past January, and it was a pretty
refreshing experience seeing how different life is over there compared to the life we live in the US.

Why I Want to Become a Web Developer

As I mentioned, I was previously employed as a sales consultant at a local Honda dealership. I had applied at Honda while I was working
the overnight shift in a warehouse at a pharmaceutical company, making a little over minimum wage, with no direction in regards to
my career. I was depressed and anti-social, and knew that in order to grow, I had to break out of my comfort zone and try something new
and I had always wanted to try working a job in sales. When I was hired, I was eager and had felt like I could've made a career out of it,
but a few months in, I started feeling burnt out, as I was working 11 hours a day, everyday, including holidays. I knew I needed to make the
leap and apply for this course to start working towards an actual career that I feel like I will enjoy for as long as I pursue it.
After not making a paycheck for 3 months due to the market conditions, as I was unable to hit my monthly quota of vehicles sold, it motivated
me to start working towards a career that will provide me the peace of mind of a guaranteed paycheck. I might not be able to make as much
as I used to, but I know I won't be spending as much time in a toxic work environment, and will have more time to spend with those I love.
